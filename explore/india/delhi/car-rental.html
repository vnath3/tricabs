Delhi - City Details

About Delhi

Delhi, officially known as the National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT), is a city and a union territory of India containing New Delhi, the capital of India. It is bordered by the state of Haryana on three sides and by Uttar Pradesh to the east. The NCT covers an area of 1,484 square kilometers (573 sq mi).

  • Population: Approximately 30 million
  • Languages Spoken: Hindi, English, Punjabi, Urdu
  • Climate: Extreme climate with hot summers and cold winters
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (INR)

Places to Visit in Delhi

  • Red Fort: A historic fort in the city that served as the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty for nearly 200 years.
  • Qutub Minar: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the tallest minarets in India, built in 1193.
  • India Gate: A war memorial located astride the Rajpath, originally called the All India War Memorial.
  • Lotus Temple: A Bahá'í House of Worship known for its flowerlike shape and stunning architecture.
  • Akshardham Temple: A Hindu temple complex known for its intricate carvings and stunning architecture.
  • Jama Masjid: One of the largest mosques in India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1644 and 1656.
  • Humayun's Tomb: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is the tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun.
